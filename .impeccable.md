## Design Context

### Users
AriaType is for people evaluating or adopting a desktop voice typing tool for daily work. They are usually deciding whether the product is trustworthy, local-first, and practical enough to fit into existing writing workflows across desktop apps.

### Brand Personality
Plain, calm, trustworthy. The interface should feel like carefully designed desktop software rather than a loud SaaS marketing site. Users should feel clarity, confidence, and control instead of pressure or spectacle.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a warm neutral palette, rounded surfaces, restrained contrast, and subtle motion. Favor editorial spacing, grouped surfaces, and explicit state presentation over decorative iconography or sales-oriented visual tricks. Keep pages aligned with the desktop product so the website feels like an extension of the app. For download and conversion moments, prefer a restrained and credible tone, and explicitly avoid generic SaaS landing-page aesthetics.

### Design Principles
1. Match the desktop product's calm, local-first visual language before introducing novelty.
2. Let typography, spacing, and grouping carry hierarchy more than illustration or ornament.
3. Make trust visible through explicit states, requirements, and control surfaces.
4. Keep conversion flows low-friction, but never pushy or sales-like.
5. Preserve accessibility and clarity with subtle motion, strong contrast, and concise copy.
